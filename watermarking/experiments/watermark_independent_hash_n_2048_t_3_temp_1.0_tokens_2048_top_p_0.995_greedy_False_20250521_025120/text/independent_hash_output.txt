 The victim is a wealthy and enigmatic businessman.
There was no marketing the Littleton mansion, because it belonged to a reclusive millionaire named Arne Magnuson. He lived alone, and his private estate on the outskirts of town was a mystery to those who passed by on the main road. The streets were narrow and winding, lined with ancient trees that leaned in, as if they were listening in on the secrets shared among the households that dotted the landscape.
Renowned detective James Marlowe stood before the massive front door of Littleton, his leather trench coat billowing behind him like a dark cloud. He knew this was it; the biggest case of his career, and it was a doozy. Arne Magnuson was a man shrouded in mystery, known only by whispers and whispers of scandalous business dealings.
As Marlowe pushed open the creaky door, a musty smell wafted out, followed by the faint scent of perfume. Step inside, and that was it. Omertà was his secret creed; the fewer who knew, the safer they were.
As he entered the foyer, a sprinkling of footprints began to appear on the wooden floors. Heavy footsteps, shuffling through the musty undertexture. They belonged to one person, with considerable purpose. The light from the antique chandeliers struggled to penetrate the densified gloom.

He followed the footprints, careful not to trigger traps left behind by the killer. Each floor required attention – an impossible puzzle to decipher. The moment hit another triumvirate of harder stone.

Chaos erupted as Marlowe reached behind to disable a heavy floor slab. A muted murmur burst through the walls as he shifted the essence of the moment. Tagged defeatedly, a newly made veneer. He propelled time at him one foot at a time.

Closet-doors quickened back into order, echoing down pivotal swing motions, intensifying Marlowe’s senses into such heightened posture.
