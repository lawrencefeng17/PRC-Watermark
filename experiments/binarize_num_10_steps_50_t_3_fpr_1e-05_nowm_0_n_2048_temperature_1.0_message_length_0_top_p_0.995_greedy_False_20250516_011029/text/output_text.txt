 The main character, detective Jameson Welles, is at the mansion to investigate the murder of his business partner, Henry Wright.
Detective Welles stepped out of his car and onto the cracked driveway of the beautiful old mansion. The dim glow of a streetlight cast long shadows across the lawn, making it seem like the house was alive, watching him. This was the infamous Ravenswood Manor, where Henry Wright, a notorious collector of rare artifacts, had been hosting dinner parties for months.

Welles's mind was focused on finding any leads, checking for any surveillance footage that might have captured the crime. He took a deep breath, shedding his bag of files and emptiness that had haunted him for weeks. This was the case of the century – his business partner was dead, and theories swirled in the minds of the investigators and the wizarding middle class about unknown forces.

As he opened the door, the key clanked against the inside, the clinking amplifying in the quiet, still mansion. Welles stepped inside, finding many shuttered rooms with oppressive silence.
