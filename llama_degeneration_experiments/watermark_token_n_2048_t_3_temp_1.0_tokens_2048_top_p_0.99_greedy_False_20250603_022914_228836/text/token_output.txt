Police Commissioner Jameson stood outside the grand entrance, his eyes scanning the overgrown lawn and twisted oak trees that surrounded the mansion. He had been called to Ravenswood Manor just hours before, and he couldn't shake the feeling that he was walking into a nightmare.

"I'll go inside," he said to his trusty sidekick, Detective Thompson. "Help me find out what happened."

Thompson nodded and stepped into the musty darkness within. The air inside was thick with the scent of decay and rot, and Jameson could hear the creaks and groans of the old wooden floorboards beneath his feet.

As they made their way deeper into the mansion, the silence was oppressive, punctuated only by the occasional scuffling of insects or creak of a rusty hinge. Jameson and Thompson moved from room to room, searching for any sign of the killer.

Their first lead took them to the study, where they found a broken glass on the floor and a single, flickering candle to die for. Jameson's gut told him that this was more than just a random act of violence – someone had been here, and they had left something behind to taunt them.
